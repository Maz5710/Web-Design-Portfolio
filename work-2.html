The Moths and Butterflies of New Zealand Trust (MBNZT) is a non-profit organisation focused on promoting a thriving moth and butterfly population in New Zealand.


The website redesign aimed to address several issues:

  • - Updating the website to reflect the organisation's name change and prominently featuring the new logo.
  • - Reviewing the content structure to reduce repetition and confusion for users.
  • - Improving accessibility and visibility of funding options to encourage donations and membership.

The planning process involved implementing the fundamentals of UX design, incorporating user feedback in the design in an iterative process to achieve the desired outcome.


Through the UX research process the objectives of the re-design were to make the brand clear, increase revenue opportunities and consolidate the website content and re-order.


The changes resulted in a new website showcasing the new logo which conveys a professional yet youthful image, along with:


  • - Content was made more engaging with a vertical scroll through the species' pages.
  • - Prominent "Donate" and "Join us" buttons were added to the carousel to emphasize the importance of funding and direct users to the appropriate pages.
  • - A "Frequently Asked Questions" section with dropdown functionality was implemented to help users easily find information.

Technologies used in the redesign included Figma for design, HTML and CSS for structure and styling, JavaScript for dynamic functionality, Bootstrap for responsive design, Sass for CSS preprocessing, and Gulp for task automation.


The tools and technologies used ensured a visually appealing and user-friendly website that effectively conveyed the mission and goals of the MBNZT while facilitating engagement and funding opportunities.
